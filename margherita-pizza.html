Classic Margherita Pizza Recipe

A traditional Neapolitan pizza topped with tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, fresh basil, salt, and extra-virgin olive oil. The simplicity of ingredients allows each flavor to shine through in this iconic Italian dish.

Preparation time

  • Total Time: Approximately 2 hours
  • Preparation: 1 hour 30 minutes (including dough rising)
  • Cooking: 10-15 minutes

Ingredients

  • For the dough:
  • 500g (4 cups) "00" flour or bread flour
  • 325ml (1⅓ cups) lukewarm water
  • 7g (2 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 10g (2 tsp) salt
  • 15ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
  • For the topping:
  • 400g (14 oz) San Marzano tomatoes, crushed
  • 250g (8 oz) fresh mozzarella, torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 30ml (2 tbsp) extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

1.

Prepare the dough: In a large bowl, dissolve yeast in lukewarm water and let sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy. Add flour, salt, and olive oil, then mix until a dough forms.

2.

Knead the dough: On a floured surface, knead the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Form into a ball.

3.

Let it rise: Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.

4.

Preheat oven: Place a pizza stone or baking sheet in the oven and preheat to the highest temperature (usually 475-500°F/250-260°C).

5.

Shape the dough: Divide the dough into 2-3 portions. On a floured surface, stretch or roll each portion into a thin circle.

6.

Add toppings: Spread crushed tomatoes over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust. Sprinkle with salt, add torn mozzarella pieces.

7.

Bake: Carefully transfer the pizza to the preheated stone or baking sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.

8.

Finish and serve: Remove from oven, top with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of olive oil. Slice and serve immediately.
