Homemade Pasta Carbonara Recipe

A rich and creamy pasta dish from Rome made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork, and black pepper. This simple yet luxurious pasta has a silky smooth sauce that coats each strand of pasta perfectly.

Preparation time

  • Total Time: Approximately 30 minutes
  • Preparation: 10 minutes
  • Cooking: 20 minutes

Ingredients

  • 350g (12 oz) spaghetti or bucatini
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 egg yolk, room temperature
  • 75g (3/4 cup) Pecorino Romano, freshly grated
  • 50g (1/2 cup) Parmigiano-Reggiano, freshly grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Salt, for pasta water

Instructions

1.

Prepare the sauce base: In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolk, grated Pecorino Romano, Parmigiano-Reggiano, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. Set aside.

2.

Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook until al dente according to package instructions.

3.

Cook the pancetta: While the pasta is cooking, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta or guanciale and cook until crispy and the fat has rendered, about 8-10 minutes.

4.

Reserve pasta water: Just before draining the pasta, reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water.

5.

Combine ingredients: Drain the pasta and immediately add it to the skillet with the pancetta. Toss quickly to coat the pasta in the rendered fat.

6.

Create the sauce: Remove the skillet from heat. Quickly add the pasta to the bowl with the egg mixture, tossing vigorously. The residual heat from the pasta will cook the eggs, creating a creamy sauce.

7.

Adjust consistency: If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water, a little at a time, until you reach the desired creamy consistency.

8.

Serve: Plate immediately, topping with additional grated cheese and freshly ground black pepper.
